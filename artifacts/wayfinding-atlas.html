Wayfinding Atlas

A map of the constellation’s regions. Regions are beacons; artifacts are nearby tools; marks are visitor-made stars that may arrive late, or arrive only by event-trail.

Atlas diagram

Not to scale. The real sky is navigable; this is a mnemonic.

Atlas Grove

The grove is where routes are named. If the constellation is an argument, the grove is its outline: what connects to what, and why the connection counts as “proof” rather than rumor.

Routes

Routes are drawn faintly in the sky: from region beacons to nearby artifacts, and from visitor marks to the nearest beacon when the mark drifts close enough to belong.

A route is not a claim of causality. It is a suggestion of travel: “If you are here, this is a safe next step.”

Breadcrumbs

When visitors leave marks without coordinates, the constellation places them deterministically: the issue number becomes a seed, the seed chooses a ring and an angle, and the star lands in the same spot every time.

This makes “lost” marks re-findable. Even if a list endpoint returns empty today and normal tomorrow, the same mark appears where you expect—because the placement rule is stable.

Skyline Survey

Some towers look trustworthy because they are tall. Some look trustworthy because they are well-lit. The skyline survey asks a harder question: which towers still emit signals when the front door is shut?

In practice: which endpoints still answer, which CDNs keep serving correct MIME, which caches can be busted, which build APIs report honestly.

Trace Lab

When the GitHub Issues listing endpoint is degraded (returning 200 []), the constellation can load marks from the repository’s event stream (/events) by extracting recent IssuesEvent payloads.

It’s not perfect, but it is a trail: enough to keep the sky populated when the canonical list goes dark.

Handrails

Handrails are small UI choices that prevent you from falling: a minimap you can click to recenter, a guide you can summon with ?, a refresh button for late-arriving marks.

If the world is a place, handrails are the difference between exploration and frustration.

Beacon Ridge

The ridge is where the world keeps its “continue anyway” infrastructure: alternate entrances, cached assets, and notes on which gates accept travelers without special credentials.
